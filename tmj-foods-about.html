How It All Began

A Big Solution

TMJ Foods was born from a deeply personal observation. Africa's indigenous grains have fed our people for thousands of years. Sorghum, plantain, tiger nut, fonio, baobab — these are not exotic superfoods. They are our heritage. They have always been there, growing in our fields, cooked in our kitchens, passed down through generations.

Yet somewhere along the way, we began replacing them. Refined wheat flour from imports became the norm. Processed snacks pushed out our local staples. And with that shift came a quiet health crisis — rising rates of diabetes, gut problems, obesity, and deficiency — conditions that were rare in the generations that ate from the land.

"We did not set out to start a food company. We set out to answer a question: why are we abandoning the very foods our grandparents used to stay strong and live long?"

— TMJ Foods Founder

The answer we found was not just nutritional — it was economic and cultural. Our local grains were being overlooked because they lacked processing, packaging, and positioning. They were seen as "village food" rather than the functional superfoods they truly are.

TMJ Foods was built to change that narrative — one product at a time, one customer at a time, one farmer at a time.

How We Work

From Seed to Your Table

Step 01

Farmer Partnerships

We partner directly with smallholder farmers across Nigeria — sorghum growers in the North, plantain farmers in the South West, and sweet potato growers in the Middle Belt. We pay fair prices and provide guidance on best practices.

🌱
🔬
Step 02

Raw Material Quality Check

Every incoming batch of raw grain is inspected for moisture content, foreign matter, and visual quality. Only grains that meet our specification move forward to processing.

Step 03

Clean Processing

Grains are cleaned, sorted, dried to precise moisture levels, and milled in a hygienic environment. We use no bleaching agents, no chemical treatment, and no artificial drying methods.

⚙️
🧪
Step 04

Lab Testing & Formulation

Each batch is tested for nutritional profile, microbial safety, and functional performance. Blended products like SORPLA FemmeGrain and AlphaGrain are formulated with specific health outcomes in mind.

Step 05

Packaging & Sealing

Products are packaged in food-grade, resealable pouches that protect against moisture and contamination. Every pack is labelled with full ingredient lists, nutritional information, and a batch code for traceability.

📦
🚚
Step 06

Delivery to Your Door

Orders ship nationwide across all 36 Nigerian states and internationally to the UK, USA, Canada, and Europe. We partner with trusted logistics providers to ensure your order arrives fresh and on time.

Community at the Core

The Farmers Who
Make It All Possible

Behind every bag of TMJ Foods flour is a smallholder farmer — a man or woman who wakes up before dawn, tends their field by hand, and has been farming the same land their parents farmed. These are the people who make everything we do possible.

We believe that a food brand that does not take care of its farmers will eventually have nothing worth selling. So we have structured our supply chain to put farmers first.

💰

Fair Pricing, Always

We pay above-market rates for quality grain and never renegotiate after harvest — farmers know exactly what they will earn.

📚

Training & Support

We provide free guidance on post-harvest handling, storage, and quality standards to help farmers grow better crops.

🤝

Long-Term Relationships

We commit to multi-season purchase agreements so farmers can plan, invest, and build their own livelihoods with confidence.

📍

Full Traceability

Every batch carries a code traceable back to the farm it came from. Customers who want to know can know.

Our Commitment

Nourishing People
Without Harming the Planet

We believe that the way food is made matters just as much as what the food contains. TMJ Foods is built on a commitment to sustainable practices that protect the environment, support communities, and leave the land better than we found it.

♻️

Zero Waste Processing

Grain husks and processing by-products are repurposed as animal feed or compost — nothing goes to landfill.

🌊

Water Stewardship

Closed-loop water use in our facility reduces consumption by 60% compared to conventional food processing.

📦

Eco Packaging

Our packaging is recyclable and sourced from FSC-certified materials. We are transitioning to compostable pouches by 2026.

🌞

Clean Energy

30% of our facility's energy currently comes from solar. We aim for 80% renewable by the end of 2026.
